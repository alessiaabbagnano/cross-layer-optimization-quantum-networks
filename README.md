# Cross-Layer Optimization for Quantum Networks  
### Routing and Scheduling under Decoherence Effects

## Author
Alessia Abbagnano  
Quantum Information – Università degli Studi di Napoli Federico II  


---

## Project Overview
This report investigates the challenges of **quantum networking and Quantum Internet architectures**, focusing on the impact of **decoherence, latency, and entanglement degradation** in distributed quantum systems.

The study addresses the joint optimization of two fundamental layers:

- **Routing**, based on entanglement-aware metrics
- **Scheduling**, focused on queue management and time-slot-based resource allocation

The goal is to analyze how cross-layer interactions affect key performance indicators such as Fidelity and Quantum Bit Error Rate (QBER).

---

## Key Topics
- Quantum Internet architectures
- Quantum repeaters and entanglement swapping
- Decoherence and noise effects
- Entanglement-aware routing (Entanglement Rate ξ)
- Scheduling policies in time-slot networks
- Trade-off between Fairness and Throughput

---

## Main Contributions

### Routing
- Analysis of entanglement-aware routing metrics
- Study of non-isotonic cost functions
- Optimal path selection via simple-path enumeration
- Evaluation of distance–complexity trade-offs

### Scheduling
- Time-slot based centralized architecture
- Interaction between routing and queue dynamics
- Impact of queueing delays on coherence time (Tch)
- Comparison of scheduling policies under fairness vs throughput constraints
